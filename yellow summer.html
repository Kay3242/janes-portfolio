01 Overview

A summer in
motion

Yellow Summer is a short animated film inspired by Queenscliff's coastal culture. By combining hand-drawn animation with real photography, the project explores the relationship between illustration and reality, creating a playful visual narrative filled with warmth, nostalgia and movement.

02 The Film

Seeing it move

The film combines illustrated animation with photographic environments to create a playful tension between reality and imagination. By placing animated characters into real coastal scenes, the project transforms familiar summer moments into a lively visual experience.

03 The Concept

Blending two
worlds

Rather than creating a fully illustrated environment, I explored how animated characters could exist naturally within real photographic spaces. This approach became the foundation of the film, allowing reality and illustration to complement one another while preserving the authentic atmosphere of Queenscliff.

04 The Research

Capturing
summer

Research focused on Queenscliff's beaches, Australian coastal culture and environmental photography. By documenting real locations, textures and everyday moments, I established a visual language that informed the colour palette, compositions and atmosphere throughout the animation.

05 The Development

From photo to
illustration

I developed a simplified illustration style using flat colours, minimal detail and bold shapes to ensure the animated elements blended seamlessly with photographic backgrounds. Character design, colour decisions and composition were refined together to maintain consistency across every scene.

06 The Process

Process at a
glance

From on-site photography and storyboarding to illustration, animation and sound design, the project evolved through an iterative creative process. Each stage built upon the last, refining the visual style and narrative to create a cohesive film that seamlessly blends illustrated characters with real-world environments.

07 The Reflection

Lessons in
motion

Creating Yellow Summer strengthened my understanding of how different design disciplines can work together to tell a story. Blending photography, illustration, animation and sound required careful planning to maintain a consistent visual language while keeping the narrative engaging. The project reinforced the importance of iteration, attention to detail and designing with emotion.
