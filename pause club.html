Pause Club

Rest is resistance.

Role
Graphic Designer
Duration
4 weeks
Tools

↓ Scroll to explore
01 Overview

Understanding
the problem

Pause Club was developed in response to increasing student burnout in Australia. The visual direction explores rest as a form of resistance, creating a brand that encourages students to slow down without guilt.

02 Process

Exploring the
identity

Every visual decision was driven by a single question: How can rest feel inviting? Rather than communicating stress, the identity embraces warmth, optimism and moments of joy. Soft curves, expressive typography and cheerful colours work together to encourage students to pause and recharge.

Building the
brand system

Every element of the identity was designed to reinforce Pause Club's message of slowing down without guilt. Cheerful illustrations, expressive typography and warm colours combine to create a visual language that feels welcoming, uplifting and instantly recognisable.

Bringing the
brand to life

A brand only becomes meaningful when it exists beyond the page. Through thoughtful applications across print, packaging and promotional materials, Pause Club transforms its visual identity into an experience that is both memorable and approachable.

04 Reflection

Final thoughts

Pause Club became an exploration of how design can communicate care without feeling clinical. Through continuous refinement, I developed an identity system that balances personality with purpose—one that is adaptable across campaigns while remaining true to its message of encouraging students to pause, rest and recharge.
