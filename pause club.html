Brand Guidelines

The primary logo application uses black on light backgrounds to maintain maximum visibility and readability across print and digital formats. The reversed white logo should only be used on dark backgrounds where the primary logo becomes visually inaccessible.

Maintain consistent proportions and spacing across all logo colour variations.

Maintain minimum clear space around the logo at all times.

Do not recreate the approved logo manually. Use only the approved logo files provided. Do not distort, rotate, skew the logo in any way.

Our secondary colour palette is used to support the primary brand colours across Pause Club’s branding system. These colours are primarily applied within backgrounds, supporting graphics, packaging, and environmental applications to create balance and reduce visual overstimulation across layouts.

The secondary palette is intended to complement the primary colours rather than compete with them, reinforcing the brand’s themes of rest, emotional care, and mental pause.

The Pause Club typography system should maintain clear visual hierarchy and accessibility across all brand applications.

Tumb Regular is reserved for logos, major headlines, and key campaign statements, while Forager Bold is used for supporting headings, titles, and product names.

Different font weights may be used depending on hierarchy, readability, and application context, while still preserving the brand’s playful and approachable visual identity.

Heavier weights such as Bold and Medium should be prioritised for headings, titles, and key statements, while lighter weights may be used sparingly for supporting information and minimal layouts.

The Pause Club graphic element system uses soft geometric forms and rounded organic shapes to reinforce the brand’s themes of emotional expression, comfort, and approachability. These elements are designed to support the character system and create visually recognisable assets across digital and physical touchpoints.

Graphic elements may be used as supporting backgrounds, decorative assets, framing devices, stickers, packaging graphics, and environmental applications throughout the brand system.

Pause Club communicates using a calm, supportive, and emotionally aware tone of voice.

Messaging should feel approachable and conversational while encouraging moments of rest, reflection, and emotional honesty within high-pressure academic environments.

Do not use colour combinations with insufficient contrast. Do not apply unapproved colours outside the official brand palette. Do not add gradients, shadows, textures, bevels, or three dimensional effects.

Do not repeat, stack, or pattern the logo in a way that reduces clarity or legibility. Do not add outlines, decorative effects, or additional graphic elements to the logo.

Timeline

These stylescapes explore different visual directions for Pause Club’s branding system through experimentation with colour, typography, illustration, and graphic language. Each direction tested different ways the brand could communicate emotional support, student wellbeing, and resistance against burnout culture.

Stylescape 3 was ultimately selected because its softer rounded forms, playful characters, and bright pastel palette created a more approachable and emotionally supportive identity.

These early sketches explore different ways Pause Club could visually communicate emotional rest, support, and resistance against burnout culture.

The sketching stage allowed different visual directions, compositions, and emotional tones to be tested before refining the final identity system.

These early logo explorations experimented with integrating emotional characters and simplified graphic forms directly into the identity system. Several variations were ultimately rejected because the overall compositions felt too clean, minimal, or visually static, reducing the playful and expressive energy central to Pause Club’s identity.
